About Gulmohar (Royal Poinciana)



Delonix regia, commonly known as Gulmohar, Royal Poinciana, or Flamboyant, is a species of flowering plant in the bean family Fabaceae, subfamily Caesalpinioideae. Native to Madagascar, it's renowned for its fern-like leaves and flamboyant display of orange-red flowers. Here is some information about Gulmohar:

Botanical Name

Delonix regia

Family

Fabaceae

Features

Noted for its striking orange-red flowers and fern-like leaves, the Gulmohar tree is a popular ornamental tree in many tropical parts of the world. It blooms over the summer, creating a vivid and colorful display.

Cultivation

Gulmohar trees thrive in tropical climates and are often grown for their aesthetic appeal. They require well-drained soil and ample sunlight to flourish.
