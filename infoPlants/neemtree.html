About Neem Tree



Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Neem, Margosa, Nimtree, or Indian Lilac, is a tree in the mahogany family Meliaceae. It's native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia and has been naturalized in tropical and subtropical areas worldwide. Here is some information about the Neem Tree:

Botanical Name

Azadirachta indica

Family

Meliaceae

Features

The Neem Tree is one of two species in the genus Azadirachta. Its fruits and seeds are the source of neem oil, widely used for its medicinal properties. The name 'Nim' is derived from the Sanskrit 'nimba'.

Cultivation

Neem trees are hardy in tropical and subtropical climates. They are valued for their drought resistance and are often grown in areas where other trees might struggle to survive.
