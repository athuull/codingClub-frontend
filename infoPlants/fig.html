About Fig Tree



The Fig, scientifically known as Ficus carica, is a small tree in the flowering plant family Moraceae, native to the Mediterranean region. It's known for its edible fruit and has been cultivated since ancient times. Here is some information about the Fig Tree:

Botanical Name

Ficus carica

Family

Moraceae

Features

The Fig Tree is a species native to the Mediterranean region, including western and southern Asia. It belongs to the genus Ficus, which contains over 800 tropical and subtropical plant species. The tree produces edible fruits known simply as figs.

Cultivation

Fig trees thrive in temperate climates and are widely grown throughout the world. They require well-drained soil and a good amount of sunlight to grow and bear fruit effectively.
